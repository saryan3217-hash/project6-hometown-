Meerut: THE city where the freedom starts

Welcome to the Zenitsu Guide of Meerut—a high-energy journey through the "Sports City of India". Whether you're charging through historical landmarks at godlike speed or slowing down to savor the city's legendary street food, this guide captures the vibrant, "thunderous" spirit of Meerut.

Top 3 activities to do at Meerut:

2 km from city center Temple The Augharnath Temple in Meerut, also known as the Kali Paltan Mandir, is the oldest Shiva temple in the city and also the most frequented. The Shiva Linga at this temple is believed to be a Swayambhu (self-manifested), making it a popular attraction among the devotees of Lord Shiva.

Tombs & Mausoleums Shapir or Shahpeer Sahab ki Dargah is a mausoleum in Meerut constructed during Mughal Era by the queen, Noor Jahan. It was built in 1628 to honour a local Muslim Hazrat Shahpeer who is said to be the teacher of the Emperor Jahangir and physician/advisor to the queen

Village Hastinapur is an ancient town which was the centre to the glorious tales of the epic Mahabharata. Situated on the banks of Ganga near Meerut in UP, Hastinapur is also the birth place of three Jain Teerthankara, and it has become an important centre of pilgrimage for the Jains.

🍱 The "Zenitsu" Appetite: Must-Try Flavours

Sweets of Legends: Don't leave without trying the famous Rewari and Gajak (jaggery and sesame sweets), especially from heritage shops like Ram Chandra Sahai on Abu Lane.

Street Food Sprints: Hariya ki Lassi: Thick, creamy lassi topped with malai at Lal Kurti. Breakfast Combos: Grab crispy Kachori and Jalebi in the Saket area or near Budhana Gate. Mughlai Delights: Head to Sadar Bazaar for melt-in-the-mouth Seekh Kebabs and Biryani.

Jain Shikanji: A refreshing spiced lemonade that is a local obsession, originally from nearby Modi Nagar.
